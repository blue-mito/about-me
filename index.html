My name is Derek. I'm thirty years old and live in San Antonio, TX with my wife and two daughters (is this a Wheel of Fortune intro?). I grew up in the polar opposite of Texas in the frozen tundra of Western New York, on the white-capped shores of Lake Ontario. Living in the Great Lakes region was incredible as a kid. I spent most of my free time fly fishing for trout and smallmouth bass (depending on the season), sailing, or playing in the peach orchards behind my house. When I wasn't outside, I was most likely reading a book, playing video games, or Dungeons and Dragons with friends.

When I graduated high school, I really had no clue of what I wanted to do for a career. I went to college--a few times--but without a clear direction, my motivation wavered. On a trip hitchhiking down the East Coast with a friend, I decided that I would consider joining the military when I returned home. After a few trips bouncing back and forth from the Air Force to Navy recruiters, I signed on the dotted line to become a Cryptologic Linguist in the USAF.

To be honest, cryptology is a bit of a misnomer for my career field. Linguist is really the word in the combination to focus on. After passing some tests, including one that measures language-learning aptitude, I was sent first to Basic Military Training, and then on to the Defense Language Institute in Monterey, CA to learn a foreign language. A year later, I left the pacific paradise of Monterey Bay fluent in my assigned language.

Fast forward ten years, and here we are today. I spent six years as a linguist and the last four as a Military Training Instructor at Basic Military Training. I now begin my adventure into tech, and hopefully a future career as a software developer, as a student at Code Fellows.

Personal Goals:

  • To get a job in tech shortly after leaving the Air Force
  • Work as a software developer for a company where I believe in their mission & vision
  • Continue growing my relationships with my wife and children, even amidst a busy schedule
  • Make friends during my Code Fellows journey
